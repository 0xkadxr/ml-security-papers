# Blogs & Online Resources

A list of blogs, websites, and online resources that regularly cover ML security, AI safety, and red-teaming topics.

---

## Individual Researchers & Practitioners

### Simon Willison's Blog
- **Website:** https://simonwillison.net/
- **Focus:** Prompt injection, LLM security, AI application security
- **Why Follow:** Simon Willison has been one of the most prolific writers on prompt injection risks since the early days of LLM deployment. His blog is an essential resource for understanding practical prompt injection threats and the challenges of building secure LLM applications.

### Lilian Weng's Blog
- **Website:** https://lilianweng.github.io/
- **Focus:** ML fundamentals, LLM safety, alignment, adversarial ML
- **Why Follow:** Excellent long-form technical summaries of ML research topics, including comprehensive overviews of adversarial attacks and LLM safety.

### Nicholas Carlini's Blog
- **Website:** https://nicholas.carlini.com/
- **Focus:** Adversarial ML, ML security, privacy attacks
- **Why Follow:** Leading adversarial ML researcher at Google DeepMind. Blog covers technical analysis of attacks and defenses.

## Company Research Blogs

### Anthropic Research Blog
- **Website:** https://www.anthropic.com/research
- **Focus:** AI safety, alignment, interpretability, constitutional AI
- **Why Follow:** Publishes cutting-edge research on AI alignment, mechanistic interpretability, and safety evaluations. Key source for understanding frontier AI safety work.

### OpenAI Safety Blog
- **Website:** https://openai.com/safety
- **Focus:** AI safety, red-teaming, alignment, model evaluations
- **Why Follow:** Covers safety research, red-teaming methodology, and safety practices for frontier models.

### Google DeepMind Safety Research
- **Website:** https://deepmind.google/safety-research/
- **Focus:** AI safety, alignment, evaluation, robustness
- **Why Follow:** Publishes research on safety evaluations, alignment techniques, and responsible AI development.

### Gray Swan AI Blog
- **Website:** https://grayswanai.com/blog
- **Focus:** LLM red-teaming, adversarial attacks on LLMs, AI security
- **Why Follow:** Founded by leading adversarial ML researchers. Covers practical LLM red-teaming and automated attack methods.

### NVIDIA AI Red Team Blog
- **Website:** https://developer.nvidia.com/blog/tag/ai-red-team/
- **Focus:** AI red-teaming, ML security in production, GPU-scale attacks
- **Why Follow:** Covers practical AI red-teaming from an infrastructure and deployment perspective.

### HuggingFace Safety & Ethics
- **Website:** https://huggingface.co/blog
- **Focus:** Open-source ML safety, model cards, responsible AI
- **Why Follow:** Covers safety practices for open-source models, safety evaluations, and community standards.

### Trail of Bits AI/ML Blog
- **Website:** https://blog.trailofbits.com/category/machine-learning/
- **Focus:** ML security auditing, vulnerability research, secure ML deployment
- **Why Follow:** Security-first perspective on ML systems. Covers practical security auditing of ML pipelines and applications.

## Community Resources

### Awesome LLM Security (GitHub)
- **Website:** https://github.com/corca-ai/awesome-llm-security
- **Focus:** Curated list of LLM security resources
- **Why Follow:** Community-maintained collection of papers, tools, and resources on LLM security.

### OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications
- **Website:** https://owasp.org/www-project-top-10-for-large-language-model-applications/
- **Focus:** LLM application security best practices
- **Why Follow:** Industry-standard security guidance for LLM applications, covering prompt injection, data leakage, and more.

### AI Safety Fundamentals
- **Website:** https://aisafetyfundamentals.com/
- **Focus:** AI safety education and curriculum
- **Why Follow:** Structured learning paths for understanding AI alignment and safety research.

### Alignment Forum
- **Website:** https://www.alignmentforum.org/
- **Focus:** AI alignment research and discussion
- **Why Follow:** Active research community discussing alignment, safety, and governance. Many key alignment ideas originate here.
